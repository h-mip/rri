Research Ethics

Nuremberg Medical Trial

  • Nazi doctors did medical experiments on concentration camp victims
  • Treatments included involuntary sterilization, inducing hypothermia, exposure to diseases and toxic chemicals, experimental organ and limb transplants, and placement in low pressure chambers
  • Victims suffered excruciating pain, mutilation, and death

Nuremberg Code

  1. Voluntary consent of the human subject is absolutely essential
  2. The experiment must yield generalizable knowledge that could not be obtained in any other way and is not random and unnecessary in nature
  3. Animal experimentation should precede human experimentation
  4. All unnecessary physical and mental suffering and injury should be avoided
  5. No experiment should be conducted if there is reason to believe that death or disabling injury will occur
  6. The degree of risk to subjects should never exceed the humanitarian importance of the problem
  7. Risks to the subjects should be minimized through proper preparations
  8. Experiments should only be conducted by scientifically qualified investigators
  9. Subjects should always be at liberty to withdraw from experiments
  10. Investigators must be ready to end the experiment at any stage if there is cause to believe that continuing the experiment is likely to result in injury, disability or death to the subject

Guatemala Syphilis Tests

  • American doctors infected nearly 700 Guatemalan prison inmates, mental patients and soldiers with venereal diseases to test effectiveness of penicillin.
  • Paid for syphilis-infected prostitutes to sleep with prisoners, and if this did not lead to infection, doctors poured bacteria onto scrapes on their bodies or injected the bacteria into their spines.
  • 1946-48

Tuskegee Syphilis Experiment

  • U.S. Public Health Service study of long-term effects of syphilis, using panel of 600 black men, most of whom were poor and uneducated, without informed consent.
  • Subjects told, inaccurately, that painful research procedures like spinal taps were treatments.
  • 400 members of panel with syphilis intentionally denied treatment.
  • Death rate for those with syphilis found to be about double that of control group.
  • 1932-72

Jewish Chronic Disease Hospital Study

  • Cancer cells injected into chronically ill patients in New York.
  • Patients told they were getting skin treatments.
  • 1963

Willowbrook Study

  • Children at Willowbrook State School for "mentally defective persons" deliberately infected with hepatitis.
  • Parents had given consent, but this was prerequisite for admission to the school.
  • 1963-66

Belmont Report

  • National Commission established by U.S. Congress in response to Tuskegee revelations.
  • Released in 1979 as set of principles and guidelines for human subjects research.
  • Key Principles: Respect for persons, Beneficence, Justice

Respect for Persons

  • Individuals should be treated as autonomous agents
  • Persons with diminished autonomy are entitled to additional protections

Beneficence

  • Do no harm
  • Maximize possible benefits and minimize possible harms

Justice

  • Treat individuals and groups fairly and equitably in terms of bearing the burdens and receiving the benefits of research.

Facebook Emotional Contagion Experiment

Facebook Experiement Summary

  • We show, via a massive (N = 689,003) experiment on Facebook, that emotional states can be transferred to others via emotional contagion, leading people to experience the same emotions without their awareness. We provide experimental evidence that emotional contagion occurs without direct interaction between people (exposure to a friend expressing an emotion is sufficient), and in the complete absence of nonverbal cues.

  • Kramer, A. D., Guillory, J. E., & Hancock, J. T. (2014). Experimental evidence of massive-scale emotional contagion through social networks. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 111(24), 8788-8790.

Facebook Experiment Methods

  • 1-week experiment with N=689,003, involving analysis of 3 million posts and 122 million words.
  • New Feed algorithm manipulated to reduce exposure to post with positive or negative emotional content.
  • All content could still be viewed on friends' walls and no direct messages were altered.
  • No text was seen by researchers.

Data Protection

Data Protection

  • the technical framework and security measures designed to guarantee that all personal data are safe from unforeseen, unintended or malevolent use.

  • European University Institute, Guide on Good Data Protection Practice in Research, May 2016

Personal Data

  • any information relating to an "identified or identifiable natural person".
  • "identifiable person" = one who can be identified directly or indirectly, "in particular by reference to an identification number or to one or more factors specific to his or her physical, physiological, mental, economic, cultural or social identity."

  • European University Institute, Guide on Good Data Protection Practice in Research, May 2016

Data Security

  • take the appropriate technical and organisational measures to prevent any unauthorised act with regard to the data;
  • make sure that no one will access, read, copy, alter, use in any way or process the data unless he/she is authorised to do so according to clear access rules;
  • organise the processing in a way that gives you complete control and tracking of the procedures followed;
  • if someone is processing data on your behalf, you need to choose someone able to guarantee secure processing.

  • European University Institute, Guide on Good Data Protection Practice in Research, May 2016

Secure Data Storage: Key Questions

  • Where is the data stored?
  • On which hardware type is the data stored?
  • Who has access to the data?
  • For how long will the data be stored, accessed?
  • If stored on a machine, is the storage machine/server equipped with Wifi, Bluetooth, USB drive, or other devices that might ease data duplication of circulation?
  • What data backup policies and processes will be implemented?

  • European University Institute, Guide on Good Data Protection Practice in Research, May 2016

H-MIP Standard Operating Procedures

  • NDAs from all team members who will have access to personal data from the project
  • No collection of personal data without informed consent
  • Abide by data use/processing limits, and requirements for handling, storage and destruction of personal data
  • All devices on which personal data from the project are stored must be encrypted and reported to PI so they can be inventoried
  • As per NDAs, personal data from the project must not be shared with third parties, including Google, Dropbox, and Slack (i.e. do not place unencrypted data in an email or on cloud storage or Slack channel)
  • Immediately report any data breaches or suspected data breaches to PI
  • Safety, privacy and other rights of yourselves and of project participants come first no matter what. Better to stop and check if any doubts.
